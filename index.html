Redesigning the User Interface of the Instagram App - a UX case study


Written by: Jessica Liu
Last Updated: January 31, 2021

In today's world, everyone has access to digital technology, including mobile and web applications. Many individuals rely on well done user interface designs in order to simplify and improve their user experience.

Instagram is a popular media sharing and social networking app available on mobile devices and desktop. Since its launch in 2010, the app has gone through many changes, including its most drastic update in November 2020. This new makeover has left many long term users upset. Due to the new changes in navigation, many users have no choice but to adjust to the new features over time.

Identifying the users and their goals


The primary users of Instagram often consist of individuals who are under the age of 35. Many of these users are most likely to be active on other social networking apps like Tiktok, Facebook, and Twitter.

The goals of Instagram users are:

  • Posting content
  • Sending and receiving messages
  • Selling products/services
  • Influencing audiences
  • Receiving information

App Analysis

This analysis will focus mainly on the story elements of Instagram. The biggest change from the 2020 Instagram update is the new placement of the media button, where users can post their content, stories, etc. In the older versions, Instagram always had their media button placed on the bottom center of the page. Since then, the reels button has taken over that spot and the media button has made it's way to the top right corner.

When deciding to post a story on Instagram, there are many ways to do so on the app. First, individuals can swipe right to get to the media page right when they open their home screen. The second option is locating the media button on the far right corner. The third option allows users to click on their profile picture to "Add Story" (not pictured). Providing multiple ways to post a story can be helpful for users. However, it can be confusing for new users or inactive users at first. Once you get on the media page, you can adjust which type of media you want to use at the bottom swipe bar. The swipe bar is noticeable small and hard to see. To click for content, users need to click on the picture box on the bottom left, which is also hard to locate at first glance.

After you click on the picture box, you'll be able to choose content from the last 24 hours or you can select a different album to choose from.



Design Problem Statement

Noticing the minor flaws in Instagram's story options, we can conclude that the main pain point of this app is that users need to adjust themselves to new navigation from the new Instagram update.

Design Question: "How might we simplify the user experience of posting a story on Instagram?"

User Research

For this case study, I conducted ten interviews and had five users walk me through their thinking process as they opened the Instagram app and posted a story.

"It's hard noticing the bottom slider once you click on the media button. I wish it was either higher or a little bigger." - Daniel John

The first thing I acknowledged was the noticeable difference between active Instagram users and inactive Instagram users. Active Instagram users didn't have a lot of trouble completing the task. However, active users noted that using the app's updated version was complicated at first, but after posting more they eventually got used to everything. On the otherhand, the inactive users had to play around more to reach the actual destination. Once they found the media button, users barely noticed the media swipe bar at the bottom.

"Last 24 Hours doesn't really help indicate anything. I don't really know why it's there." - Jamie Lee

Another feature that stood out was the select button that said "Last 24 Hours". A couple users preferred the button to say "Recents" or "Select" to make it more straightforward.

The Solution

Based on participant feedback and interview highlights, I created an improved story design for Instagram to provide its users an easier way to post a story.

Enlarged Media Swipe Bar and Modified Select Button

This new design slightly adjusts Instagram's current design by making the media swipe bar more noticeable to its users. Although small, this new adjustment solves a big problem by simplifying the process of finding the media swipe bar for new or inactive users.

The modified select button matches the iPhone "Recents" album, making it easier to know what the button is intended for.
